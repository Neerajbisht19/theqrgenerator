UPI QR Code for Shops: The Complete Guide to Accept Payments (2026)

The QR Generator·June 2026·8 min read

India has completely transitioned into a digital-first economy. For a shop owner, whether you run a small kirana store, a busy salon, or a large retail outlet, accepting cash is no longer enough. The fastest, safest, and most cost-effective way to get paid is by using a custom UPI QR code.

Why Every Shop Needs a Custom UPI QR Code

While banks offer their own printed standees, generating your own branded UPI code has distinct advantages. If you want to learn the broader concepts of digital transactions, you can read our deep dive into how QR codes for payments work.

  • Zero MDR (Merchant Discount Rate): Unlike credit card swipe machines (POS) that charge 1.5% to 2% per transaction, UPI payments are absolutely free for merchants.
  • Universal Compatibility: A single UPI QR code works flawlessly across all apps. You don't need separate codes; a customer can scan it using Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, or BHIM. Read more about universal UPI compatibility here.
  • Instant Settlement: The money is transferred directly from the customer's bank account to your bank account in seconds, ensuring continuous cash flow.

How to Create a UPI QR Code for Your Shop

You don't need technical skills or a bank representative to get started. You can generate a high-quality, scannable code right now using our free UPI QR Code Generator.

  1. Find your exact UPI ID (Virtual Payment Address or VPA). It usually looks like yourmobilenumber@bankname or shopname@upi.
  2. Open our UPI tool and enter your UPI ID.

Pro Tip for Small Businesses: Printing your code on a sturdy acrylic standee protects it from wear and tear. For more growth tips, check out our guide on how small businesses are using QR codes to grow.

Fixed Amount vs. Open Amount UPI QR Codes

When you use a generator, you have the option to leave the amount blank or pre-fill it. This is basically the difference between Static and Dynamic QR approaches for payments:

Open Amount (Static): Best for grocery stores, hardware shops, and general retail where every customer's bill is different. The customer scans, types the bill amount, and pays.

Fixed Amount (Dynamic Feel): If you sell a fixed-price service (e.g., a car wash that costs exactly ₹500, or an entry ticket), you can embed the amount into the QR code. When the customer scans, the ₹500 is already filled in, preventing typing errors and speeding up the line.

Specific Use Cases for Retailers

Different types of shops can leverage UPI QR codes in unique ways to improve customer experience:

1. Restaurants and Cafes

Instead of making customers wait at the cash register, print a UPI QR code on the final bill receipt. The customer can scan and pay right from their table. If you want to digitize your menu as well, learn how to create a QR code for restaurant menus.

2. Salons and Spas

Place a beautifully designed QR code near the mirror or reception. Customers can pay contactlessly while their service is finishing up. We have a dedicated guide on optimizing QR codes for salons.

3. Delivery Businesses

If your shop offers home delivery, give your delivery boys a printed UPI QR code badge. Customers who don't have cash at the door can instantly scan the boy's badge and pay the exact bill amount securely.

Security Best Practices for Shop Owners

While UPI is highly secure, shop owners must stay vigilant against common frauds:

  • Always verify the SMS: Never rely solely on the customer showing you a "Payment Successful" screen. Always wait for the SMS confirmation from your bank or use a smart speaker (like Paytm Soundbox) to verify the receipt of funds.
  • Check for physical tampering: Check your printed QR code daily to ensure no scammer has pasted their own QR sticker over yours. Read more about preventing these scams in our ultimate guide to QR code security.

Conclusion

In 2026, a shop without a UPI QR code is losing customers every single day. Generating your own code is fast, free, and gives you total control over how your brand is presented at the checkout counter. Don't wait on banks to send you a generic standee—take control of your payments today.
