What is a UPI QR Code?

A UPI QR code encodes your UPI Virtual Payment Address (VPA) so customers can pay you by scanning — instead of typing your UPI ID manually. It supports GPay, PhonePe, Paytm, Amazon Pay, BHIM, and every other UPI-enabled app in India.

Fixed Amount vs Open Amount

Leave the amount blank for an open-amount QR — the customer enters the amount. Set a fixed amount for specific invoices or products. Fixed-amount QRs are useful for street food stalls, parking, or any fixed-price items.

Where to Use Your UPI QR

  • Shop counter — Print and laminate. A4 size works well.
  • Delivery packaging — Add to bags for repeat customers.
  • Visiting cards — Freelancers and consultants.
  • Invoices — Embed QR in PDF invoices.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

All UPI-compliant apps: GPay, PhonePe, Paytm, Amazon Pay, BHIM, Mobikwik, FreeCharge, and any bank UPI app. The QR uses the standard upi:// protocol.
You can set a fixed amount that pre-fills in the payment app, but most UPI apps allow the user to modify it before confirming. For controlled amounts, use payment gateway links instead.
Verify your UPI ID is correct and active. Use the Large or Print size for physical prints. Print at minimum 3×3 cm. Ensure good lighting when scanning. Low contrast or blurry prints reduce reliability.
UPI payments between individuals are free. Business transactions may have charges depending on your payment service provider. The QR code itself has no fee.
