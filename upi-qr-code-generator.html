Free UPI QR Code Generator: Accept Digital Payments Instantly

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) has revolutionized how India transacts. A customized UPI QR Code securely encodes your Virtual Payment Address (VPA), allowing customers to pay you directly from their smartphones. Instead of asking customers to manually type your phone number or UPI ID—which often leads to payment errors—they simply scan, authenticate, and pay.

How to Create Your Custom UPI QR Code

  1. Enter Your UPI ID (VPA): This is the most crucial step. Input your exact Virtual Payment Address (e.g., yourname@paytm, 9876543210@ybl, or yourshop@sbi). Double-check the spelling to ensure payments are routed to the correct bank account.
  2. Add Merchant Name (Optional): Entering your shop or business name builds trust, as the customer will see this name on their screen before confirming the transaction.
  3. Set a Fixed Amount & Note (Optional): If you are selling a fixed-price item, you can enter the exact amount in rupees. You can also add a payment note (like an invoice number) to easily track the transaction in your bank statement.
  4. Download & Print: Customize the color, select the Print size (500px), and download the QR code in high resolution.

Fixed Amount vs. Open Amount QR Codes

Open Amount QR: Leave the amount field completely blank if you run a general store, grocery shop, or restaurant. The customer will scan the code, manually enter their total bill amount, and complete the payment.

Fixed Amount QR: Setting a fixed amount is incredibly useful for standard-priced services, entry tickets, charity donations, parking fees, or specific invoices. While most UPI apps allow the user to modify the amount if needed, a pre-filled amount significantly reduces manual typing errors and speeds up the checkout line.

Where to Display Your UPI QR Code

  • Shop Billing Counters: Print, laminate, and place it directly at your cash register (A4 or A5 size works best).
  • Digital Invoices: Embed the QR code directly into your PDF invoices. Clients can scan the screen with their phone to settle pending bills instantly.
  • Delivery Packaging: Add the QR code to your delivery boxes or carry bags as an easy, contactless alternative to Cash on Delivery (COD).
