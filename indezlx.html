What is a QR Code and Why Does Color Matter?

A QR code is a two-dimensional matrix barcode that smartphones can scan to trigger a specific action — opening a website, starting a payment, connecting to WiFi, or opening an app. The pattern of dark and light modules encodes data that your phone's camera decodes in milliseconds.

Color matters enormously for scannability. The higher the contrast between the dark modules and the white background, the faster and more reliably any QR scanner can read it. Pure black (#000000) on white is the gold standard — it works in bright sunlight, dim indoor lighting, and on glossy printed surfaces. Lighter colors reduce contrast and can cause scan failures, especially in difficult lighting conditions. QRBanao generates jet-black QR codes by default for exactly this reason.

What Are Deep Link QR Codes?

Most QR generators simply encode a plain URL or text string. Deep links are specially formatted URLs and data strings that tell the operating system to open a specific app and perform a specific action. QRBanao uses deep links for every supported type — so scanning a WhatsApp QR opens WhatsApp directly (not a browser), scanning a UPI QR opens Google Pay with your payment details pre-filled, and scanning a WiFi QR connects the phone automatically without any manual steps.

PNG vs SVG — Which to Download?

Both formats are available for every QR code, completely free.

  • PNG — for digital use: WhatsApp, email, websites, social media. Choose larger sizes for better digital display.
  • SVG — for print: visiting cards, banners, menus, packaging. Scales infinitely without pixelation.
  • When in doubt — download both. Both are clean with zero watermark.
